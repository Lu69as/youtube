This is the story of my YouTube channel

2017

Just like Jesus - LukasTrainer

Just like Jesus, my channel LukasTrainer was created on December 25th—but instead of miracles, my first upload was an unboxing of the Zelda Monopoly game I got for Christmas. I even got my dad to make an intro using Owl City’s Fireflies. A few days later, on New Year’s Eve, I recorded my first gaming video, playing Goat Simulator using a setup made of cardboard, a selfie stick, and my phone (no mic, no capture card, just vibes). I was too young to upload without permission, so everything went through my parents first.

2018

Bragging at school

Back at school after New Year’s, I bragged about my channel—and surprisingly, everyone loved it. I guess growing up with YouTube made it instantly cool. Like everyone else, I eventually jumped on the Fortnite train, and classmates literally begged me to stream it—so I did, even making my grandparents try it once. Around the same time, I dove into vlogging, filming pretty much everything I did—whether it was shopping in Sweden or running around a restaurant playzone during a family dinner.

2019

The end of an era

Through 2018 and early 2019, I kept making small gaming videos and vlogs, though less frequently, as my interests shifted toward English-speaking YouTubers. Just before summer break and the end of elementary school, I rebranded my channel to F0RR34L and privated all my old videos. But during a sleepover at my grandpa’s house, my sister—unknowingly using my account on his tablet—found and watched my videos. Thinking YouTube’s private setting had failed, I panicked and deleted everything. I’ve regretted it since, as those cringey childhood videos are now lost forever—a big piece of my creative history gone for good.

2020

A new beginning - F0RR34L

Starting secondary school marked a fresh start for me and my YouTube channel, now rebranded as F0RR34L. The first video in this new era? Minecraft sheep torture, obviously. I finally figured out how to record and edit on my PlayStation, but let’s be honest: my early content mostly consisted of me saying “bruh” to everything. Christmas 2019 brought the glorious switch to PC, unlocking better video quality and weirder content like No, Yes and my dog eats you. Then came 2020—and with it, a pandemic, a beat from a podcast, and my first viral hit: a Coronavirus song that hit 2k views in one day. Later that year, I released the painfully awkward Gamin with a girl, and just before Christmas, I dropped Cringe Christmas , a track that, while chaotic, was only a warm-up for what was coming next.

2021

A new beginning, again? - Lu69as

In 2021, my content finally started to feel genuinely good. I found my sense of humor, developed a stronger editing style, and rebranded once again—this time to Lu69as, a name that stuck (at least for now). This era brought weird and wild experiments like roleplaying a scared soldier in Pavlov WW2 or diving into the newest Flåklypa game. I also hit my first real milestone: 100 subscribers, something I celebrated with full enthusiasm. While I still struggled with staying consistent, this was the year I truly felt like a creator.

2023

I am lightning

I was starting high school when a friend convinced me to buy a steering wheel which, let’s just say, kinda took over the channel for a bit. I went full throttle into racing content, and I mean only racing content. I made video's like conquering the Nürburgring with a random input "AI", and recorded my journey through Iracing's rookie series, This became the most consistent and focused my channel had ever been, and while the randomness took a slight back seat, I had finally found a niche that pushed my creativity in a new direction.

2024

I'm just Lukas

After months of pure racing content, I began experimenting again. While iRacing remained central—with long-haul races, dramatic challenges, and dragging friends into my sim-racing obsession, I also branched out a bit. I reviewed Doki Doki Literature Club, played Lethal Company with a steering wheel, and released I'm Just Lukas, a magnificent parody of I'm Just Ken that blew the music world up with it's quality and creativity.
